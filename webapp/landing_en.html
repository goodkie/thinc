Core Technology & Scientific Foundations

🌀

Laryngeal Micro-Tremor Index (MTI)

When a speaker experiences emotional strain or cognitive dissonance (deception), autonomic nervous responses involuntarily increase tension in the vocal tract muscles. This suppresses the natural 8–12 Hz micro-tremors present during stress-free speech. MTI monitors this frequency band at a high-resolution spectrum level.

⚡

Teager Energy Operator (TEO)

Unlike traditional amplitude measurements, the non-linear Teager Energy Operator captures the physical airflow energy profile inside the vocal tract. Under cognitive load, it extracts non-linear energy surges and deformation features to flag high-stress intervals.

📊

Formant Instability Index (FI)

Vocal tract physical adjustments shift the primary formant resonance bands (F1: 300–1000 Hz, F2: 1000–3500 Hz). The Formant Instability (FI) coefficient monitors frame-by-frame dispersion anomalies to trace the acoustic distortion typical of intentional deceit.

📈

Pitch Deviation Ratio (PDR)

Natural dialog patterns maintain a fluid pitch variance. Under high cognitive overload, brief micro-spasms produce sudden pitch jumps. PDR tracks these momentary anomalies and integrates pitch variance directly into the stress assessment matrix.
